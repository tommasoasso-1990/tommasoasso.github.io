Bio

Born in Florence in 1990, I am an Italian architect and painter fascinated by the many lives of cities. My work moves between technical precision and emotional intuition, constructing imaginary urban landscapes through layers of color, architectural fragments and human presence.

For me, the city is also a mental space — a place made of memory and imagination. My paintings do not depict real places; they stage the experience of inhabiting them. The city becomes a symbol of collective memory and personal perception.

Alongside these urban imaginaries, a recurring figure appears: the rooster. A timeless companion to humankind, it embodies energy and resilience — a primal presence at the threshold between nature and culture. Cities speak of civilization; the rooster recalls what came before. Together, they build imagined worlds that point to something universal.
